A lesson to be learned

Life can bring many challenges. It is important to keep a cool head. I see people get agitated easily. Maybe something didn't go as they had hoped. Plans did not end up meeting expectations. It can be very disheartening, especially if you've waited for it. Waited a long time.

But what I have learned it: It's not worth getting upset about. That is to say, that eventually, life will come down much harder on you, than you'd imagine. I have learned this the hard way. Sometimes there really is a phase, where it seems there is no light at the end of the tunnel. And if I weren't your friend (I mean I think we're friends anyway) I'd tell you "cheer up, it'll get better".

The hard (and much more disturbing) truth is though, that I don't know that. I can't promise you anything will get better. Maybe you're diagnosed with cancer one day, inoperable. How could I tell you things will get better, when there simply is no capacity for things to get better? I can't. And I won't. I've seen enough of that to know.

Something to remember

One day you might find yourself regretting a decision you have made. Maybe you find yourself regretting many decisions you made. Potentially you will find yourself consumed by sorrow and regret, over things you think you've done and get restless. I have been there. Consider where these decisions came from. Chances are, they came from good intention. If they did not play out as you thought, you still have something going for you. As much as it seems they have ruined your life, you did them with good intention in said case. It doesn't mean that the situation you're in gets any better. But maybe you can accept that even if it's something that went wrong, it wasn't malicious. If you succeed in that, time can heal such wound.
